2024-11-10

I've gotten used to life in the US. Prices don't feel insane, portions are manageable (although still too large), and I've adjusted to the way people talk to each other.
The past 7 weeks have been just exciting. I visited the tourist-y spots of San Francisco, New Haven (I loved Yale campus), and Boston. I've learned more than I have in the past few years combined. I've picked up authors like William James (many thoughts about him), and have read classics like Homer's Odyssey and Sophocles' Antigone.
I've also re-learned the basic concepts of programming. Be it the basic types of data like lists, arrays, dictionaries, going over them has refreshed my memory and has made me excited to learn more in-depth. Having spent the past few years not really learning what I wanted to, this is a nice change of topic, to something I'm actually invested in learning.
I've started to do debate again - purely as a hobby, and in two completely new formats (BP and APDA). Learning these has been challenging, and I feel like I'm much less skilled than the others around me, but it's not discouraged me. On the contrary, seeing the skill of people here has made it so much more fun to push myself to do more. I've also started to play North American Taiko - a mix of traditional Japanese wadaiko and American influences like blues and jazz. As something completely new to me, it's exciting and has shown me the fun of percussion instruments.

2024-09-10

Writing this on my plane to San Francisco; maybe it's the fact that Americans are, on average, much bigger than I am, but this United flight feels more spacious than I'm used to. The Boeing 777 might be my new favourite plane to ride!
Praying to the ancestors of both sides of my family before departing, I realised just how religious I can be, even when I am a self-described agnostic, usually non-religious. What is considered religious in the West might be considered superstitious in Japan, but praying to ancestors probably counts as a religious act, especially when it's in front of a proper Buddhist altar. Maybe what I consider to be superstitious might just be an extremely decentralized religion? Anyways, I prayed for my ancestors to look over me and to protect my luggage from being damaged on my flight. Here's hoping they answer my prayers (I don't know if that's how it works).
I've noticed how I've been finding it more and more difficult to sleep on flights recently, I hope I can find a way to overcome this, or my future travel plans might be in jeopardy. Something intersting I've noticed is how I feel sleepier with hyperflip and other louder genres than relaxing music. Maybe the auditory overstimulation might actually help me sleep? Regardless, I've got to get myself an eye mask and some melatonin (or is it melanin?) for my next flight.

2024-09-09

Another full month of no updates... I might just be bad at blogging.
August was full of events; I embarked on a two-week trip to the People's Republic of China, visiting 5 cities (Shanghai, Wuzhen, Hangzhou, Nanjing, Bejing) and gorging myself on the delicious food available. The image above is of the Great Wall of China, which was more tiring than expected to walk through. I didn't realize it would be so vertical, but it is, after all, built on top of mountains.
I also went on a 9-hour, 100km trip around Lake Kasumigaura, the second-largest lake in Japan. Exhausting, but really fun to do with friends; I'd recommend going early in the morning so you don't have to worry about the sun setting.
Packing my personal belongings right now to prepare for my trip to the US, I'm somewhat surprised that all my belongings fit in 2 suitcases + my guitar.

2024-07-30

I've been going outside a lot recently, whether it be to the CLAMP exhibition (pic related), the graves of relatives, or a friend's DJ event.
The CLAMP exhibition was amazing, with so many original color illustrations and manga panels on show. I was able to see artwork from my favourite CLAMP works like Cardcaptor Sakura, Chobits, and Kobato, along with a few illustrations from related works like Code Geass.
Going to the cemetery was a relaxing experience, talking with relatives about the time spent with (recently) deceased family members. The cemetery itself was beautiful and quiet, with a relaxing atmosphere, even in the Tokyo summer.
I'd never been to a DJ event before, and although it was in a relatively small club, the flashing lights and the loud music was very overstimulating. I really enjoyed it, and I'd love to go again.
